# Project Journal

This journal documents the design, construction, wiring, testing, and documentation of my model rocket launch controller.

## Total Time

**Approximately 5 hours**

---

## Planning the Project

**Time spent: 45 minutes**

I planned the overall layout and function of the launch controller. I decided to use several separate safety controls instead of allowing the launch button to work by itself.

The planned controls included:

* Key-lock safety switch
* Covered arming switch
* Momentary safety button
* Continuity indicator LED
* Buzzer
* Large red launch button

The main goal was to add as many buttons as possible while following safety regulations 

---

## Wiring planning

**Time spent: 1 hour 15 minutes**

I worked on the first wiring plan and schematic for the controller.

The hardest part was determining the correct order of the controls. I needed the launch output to work only after all required safety switches were active. I also needed the continuity indicator to check the igniter connection without activating the igniter.

Pretty much, I had to figure out the amps needed to set the ignitor off so that I could purposley flow the amount of amps to not set the igniters off before the actual safetys were turned off. 

---

## Session 3 — CAD designing

**Time spent: 1 hour**

I designed the controller enclosure to hold the switches, buttons, indicator LED, buzzer, wiring, and launch leads.

I arranged the controls so that it had a certain order to it, and you could not mess it up as much. 

It was a pretty simple design, just a box with some emblems on it. 



---

## Session 4 — Assembly 

**Time spent: 1 hour 50 minutes**

I installed the controls into the enclosure and completed the internal wiring.

This stage required fitting the wires and components into the case while keeping the connections organized. I checked the switch order and verified that the launch button could not operate without the required safety controls.

wiring was so much fun that, I had to un solder so many times do to wirings touching, or I just straight up connected the wrong ones as the shematic was complicated.  Launch leds was a stripped extenstion cord. The third wire is just a signal wire or sm, its there for the love of the game. 
---

## Session 5 — Testing and Launch Demonstration

**Time spent: 10 minutes**

I tested the completed controller and checked the safety sequence.

The testing included:

* Checking the key-lock switch
* Checking the covered arming switch
* Testing the momentary safety button
* Checking continuity feedback
* Testing the buzzer
* Testing the final launch button

The controller was then demonstrated during a real model rocket launch. The video shows the safety controls being used and the controller successfully completing the launch.

---

## Final Result

The final project is a completed and functional model rocket launch controller with layered physical safety controls, continuity feedback, audible feedback, a custom enclosure, and a working launch demonstration.

The project taught me alot about circuity, cad, and wiring, but lowkey was more about patience, and if something works DO NOT TOUCH IT.
